Greeting Customers & Taking Orders (손님 맞이 및 주문받기)

Today's Goal: Greet customers naturally and take their food or drink orders.
(오늘의 목표: 손님에게 자연스럽게 인사하고 음식이나 음료 주문받기)

Photo: A friendly barista taking an order from a customer at a cafe counter.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever worked in a cafe or restaurant before?

    (이전에 카페나 식당에서 일해본 적이 있나요?)

  • What is the first thing you should say when a customer walks in?

    (손님이 들어올 때 가장 먼저 해야 할 말은 무엇일까요?)

  • Why is smiling so important in customer service?

    (고객 서비스에서 미소가 그토록 중요한 이유는 무엇일까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Hi, how are you doing today?

    (안녕하세요, 오늘 기분이 어떠신가요?)

  • Are you ready to order?

    (주문하시겠습니까?)

  • What can I get for you?

    (무엇을 준비해 드릴까요?)

  • Would you like that for here or to go?

    (매장에서 드실 건가요, 아니면 포장해 가실 건가요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Take your time. Let me know when you're ready. (천천히 고르세요. 준비되시면 알려주세요.)

  • Can I get you anything else with that? (그것과 함께 다른 것도 준비해 드릴까요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia, New Zealand, and the UK, people say "Takeaway" or "Have here". In the US and Canada, it is "To go" or "For here".

  • Always ask "How are you?" before asking what they want to order. It's polite culture!

Explaining the Menu & Recommendations (메뉴 설명 및 추천하기)

Today's Goal: Describe menu items and confidently recommend food to customers.
(오늘의 목표: 메뉴 항목을 설명하고 고객에게 자신감 있게 음식 추천하기)

Photo: A server pointing at a menu and warmly explaining a dish to customers.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What do you say when a foreigner asks "What's good here?"

    (외국인이 "여기 뭐가 맛있어요?"라고 물으면 뭐라고 대답하시나요?)

  • How do you describe your favorite Korean food?

    (가장 좋아하는 한국 음식을 어떻게 설명하시겠어요?)

  • Why is it important for staff to know the menu well?

    (직원이 메뉴를 잘 아는 것이 왜 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Our most popular dish is ___.

    (저희의 가장 인기 있는 요리는 ___입니다.)

  • I highly recommend the ___.

    (저는 ___을 강력히 추천합니다.)

  • It comes with ___.

    (이것은 ___와 함께 제공됩니다.)

  • It's a bit spicy / sweet / salty.

    (이건 약간 맵습니다 / 답니다 / 짭니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • It's my personal favorite. (제가 개인적으로 가장 좋아하는 메뉴입니다.)

  • It's very filling, so it's good for sharing. (양이 아주 든든해서 나눠 먹기 좋습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Customers love it when you say "It's my personal favorite." It builds trust instantly.

  • Be ready to describe *what* is in the food (e.g., "It comes with fries and a salad").

Handling Special Requests (특별 요청 및 알레르기 대처하기)

Today's Goal: Listen to dietary requirements and modify orders correctly.
(오늘의 목표: 식단 요구사항(알레르기 등)을 듣고 주문을 올바르게 수정하기)

Photo: A server carefully writing down specific instructions from a customer.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you know common food allergies in Western countries?

    (서양 국가에서 흔한 음식 알레르기를 알고 계신가요?)

  • What do you say if a customer asks for milk alternatives (like Oat milk)?

    (손님이 귀리 우유 같은 대체 우유를 요청하면 어떻게 말하시겠어요?)

  • Why is it dangerous to guess if you don't know the ingredients?

    (재료를 모를 때 짐작으로 대답하는 것이 왜 위험할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Does anyone have any allergies?

    (알레르기 있는 분 계신가요?)

  • We can make it without ___.

    (저희는 ___을 빼고 만들어 드릴 수 있습니다.)

  • Would you like regular milk or oat milk?

    (일반 우유로 하시겠어요, 아니면 귀리 우유로 하시겠어요?)

  • I'll double-check with the chef.

    (제가 주방장에게 다시 한번 확인해 보겠습니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Yes, we offer gluten-free options. (네, 글루텐 프리 옵션을 제공합니다.)

  • I will make a note of that for the kitchen. (주방에 그 점을 메모해 전달하겠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Food allergies (nuts, dairy, gluten, shellfish) are taken very seriously. Never guess—always say "I'll double-check."

  • Alternative milks (Soy, Almond, Oat) are standard in almost every cafe abroad.

Dealing with Customer Complaints (고객 불만 처리하기)

Today's Goal: Stay calm and professional when a customer is unhappy.
(오늘의 목표: 고객이 불만을 제기할 때 침착하고 프로페셔널하게 대처하기)

Photo: A server listening patiently to a customer who seems slightly upset.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What do you do if a customer says their food is cold?

    (고객이 음식이 차갑다고 하면 어떻게 하시나요?)

  • Have you ever had to deal with a very angry person?

    (매우 화가 난 사람을 상대해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Why is it important to apologize first, even if it's not your personal fault?

    (개인적인 잘못이 아니더라도 먼저 사과하는 것이 중요한 이유는 무엇일까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm so sorry about that.

    (그 점에 대해 정말 죄송합니다.)

  • Let me fix that for you right away.

    (제가 즉시 해결해 드리겠습니다.)

  • I'll bring you a new one.

    (새것으로 가져다드리겠습니다.)

  • I'll speak to the manager about this.

    (이 문제에 대해 매니저에게 말씀드리겠습니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Thank you for letting us know. (알려주셔서 감사합니다.)

  • I apologize for the delay. (지연된 점 사과드립니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • When a customer complains, don't make excuses (e.g., "We are very busy"). Just apologize and offer a solution.

  • If a customer gets too aggressive, step away and say "I'll get my manager."

  • Is it hard not to take customer complaints personally?(고객의 불만을 개인적인 감정으로 받아들이지 않는 것이 어렵나요?)

Tutor Tip

• Emphasize the three steps of handling a complaint: Apologize -> Take Action -> Thank them for telling you.

• "Let me fix that" sounds proactive and caring.

Payment & Closing the Sale (결제 및 마무리 인사)

Photo: A cashier handing a receipt to a customer with a smile.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How do you politely ask for payment?

    (결제를 요청할 때 어떻게 정중하게 말하시나요?)

  • What happens when customers want to pay separately (split the bill)?

    (손님들이 따로 결제(더치페이)하고 싶어 할 때 어떤 일이 벌어지나요?)

  • Why is the last interaction with a customer so important?

    (고객과의 마지막 상호작용이 왜 그렇게 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Are you paying together or separately?

    (같이 결제하시나요, 따로 하시나요?)

  • How would you like to pay?

    (결제는 어떻게 도와드릴까요?)

  • Cash or card?

    (현금이신가요, 카드이신가요?)

  • Here is your receipt.

    (여기 영수증입니다.)

  • Have a great day!

    (좋은 하루 보내세요!)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • You can just tap your card here. (여기에 카드를 대시면 됩니다.)

  • Would you like a copy of your receipt? (영수증 사본 필요하신가요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia and NZ, "Pay Wave" or "Pay Pass" (tapping the card) is standard. "Tap your card" is the phrase you'll use 100 times a day.

  • Always smile when handing over the receipt and say "Have a good one!" or "Have a great day!"
