Immigration: Stating Your Purpose (입국 심사: 방문 목적 명확히 말하기)

Today's Goal: Answer 3 immigration questions calmly and clearly.
(오늘의 목표: 3가지 입국 심사 질문에 침착하고 명확하게 대답하기)

Photo: A traveler showing their passport and visa documents at border control.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever felt nervous at airport immigration? What was the hardest part?

    (공항 입국 심사에서 긴장해 본 적이 있나요? 가장 어려웠던 점은 무엇이었나요?)

  • Which country and city are you going to for your working holiday?

    (워킹홀리데이를 위해 어느 나라, 어느 도시로 가시나요?)

  • What is one thing you want to do in your first week abroad?

    (해외에서의 첫 주에 가장 하고 싶은 일은 무엇인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm here on a working holiday visa.

    (저는 워킹홀리데이 비자로 왔습니다.)

  • I plan to stay for about a year.

    (약 1년 정도 머물 계획입니다.)

  • I'll be staying at an Airbnb in Sydney for the first week.

    (첫 주 동안은 시드니의 에어비앤비에 머물 예정입니다.)

  • Here is the address.

    (여기 주소가 있습니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I'm still finalizing my plans, but I have accommodation booked for the first two weeks. (아직 계획을 세우는 중이지만, 처음 2주간의 숙소는 예약해 두었습니다.)

  • I can show my bank statement if needed. (필요하다면 은행 잔고 증명서를 보여드릴 수 있습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • "Working Holiday Visa (subclass 417/462)" and "proof of funds (bank statement)" are commonly asked by immigration officers.

  • Keep key details ready on your phone: address, booking, visa info, and contact number.

Transportation: Airport to Accommodation (공항에서 숙소로 이동하기)

Today's Goal: Share your address and handle a route issue politely.
(오늘의 목표: 주소를 공유하고 경로 문제를 정중하게 처리하기)

Photo: A traveler loading heavy bags into the back of an Uber or taxi.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How are you planning to get to your accommodation from the airport?

    (공항에서 숙소까지 어떻게 이동할 계획이신가요?)

  • Have you ever gotten lost in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 길을 잃어본 적이 있나요?)

  • What part of settling in feels hardest to you?

    (초기 정착 과정에서 가장 어렵게 느껴지는 부분은 무엇인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Where can I catch a taxi / an Uber?

    (택시나 우버를 어디서 탈 수 있나요?)

  • Can you take me to this address, please?

    (이 주소로 가주시겠어요?)

  • About how long will it take?

    (시간이 대략 얼마나 걸릴까요?)

  • Could you speak a bit more slowly, please?

    (조금만 더 천천히 말씀해 주시겠어요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Could you please follow the GPS? (내비게이션(GPS)을 따라가 주시겠어요?)

  • Could we avoid the toll road, please? (유료 도로는 피해서 가주실 수 있나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In English-speaking countries, clear and simple communication is appreciated, especially at counters and with drivers.

  • Rideshare apps (Uber, Didi) are often cheaper and easier to use directly from the airport's 'Rideshare Pickup' zone.

Banking: Opening an Account (은행: 계좌 개설하기)

Today's Goal: Open an account and ask about fees and card delivery.
(오늘의 목표: 은행 계좌를 개설하고 수수료와 카드 배송일에 대해 묻기)

Photo: A customer sitting at a desk with a bank teller, handing over documents.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you know any major banks in your working holiday country?

    (가고자 하는 국가의 주요 은행 이름을 알고 계신가요?)

  • What do you think you need to open a bank account?

    (계좌를 개설하려면 어떤 것들이 필요하다고 생각하시나요?)

  • Why do you need a local bank account quickly?

    (현지 은행 계좌가 왜 빨리 필요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to open a bank account.

    (은행 계좌를 개설하고 싶습니다.)

  • What documents do I need?

    (어떤 서류가 필요한가요?)

  • Is there an account-keeping fee?

    (계좌 유지 수수료가 있나요?)

  • How long does it take to get a debit card?

    (체크카드를 받는 데 얼마나 걸리나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Is there an account with no monthly fees for working holiday makers? (워킹홀리데이 메이커를 위한 월 수수료 없는 계좌가 있나요?)

  • I haven't received my TFN yet. Is that okay? (아직 TFN(납세 번호)을 받지 못했는데 괜찮은가요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Your tax number is called a "TFN" in Australia, "SIN" in Canada, and "NIN" in the UK.

  • You can say: "I haven't received my TFN yet—can I still open an account?"

LESSON 3: Banking - Wrap-up & Assignment

6. Talk More

  • Do you usually use cash or card in daily life?(일상생활에서 주로 현금을 쓰시나요, 카드를 쓰시나요?)

  • What bank services are most important to you: fees, app, or ATMs?(수수료, 앱, ATM 중 어떤 은행 서비스가 가장 중요한가요?)

Tutor Tip

• Teach the difference between 'debit card' (checks) and 'credit card' (borrows).

• "Account-keeping fee" is a vital phrase as many Western banks charge monthly fees unless you ask for an exemption.

SIM Card: Getting a Phone Number (유심 카드: 전화번호 만들기)

Today's Goal: Choose a plan, decline politely, and ask for a cheaper option.
(오늘의 목표: 요금제를 선택하고, 정중하게 거절하며, 더 저렴한 옵션 묻기)

Photo: Someone holding a smartphone and looking for a network signal.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How much mobile data do you usually use per month?

    (한 달에 모바일 데이터를 보통 얼마나 사용하시나요?)

  • Do you prefer prepaid or postpaid (contract) plans?

    (선불 요금제와 후불(약정) 요금제 중 어떤 것을 선호하시나요?)

  • What do you use your phone for most while traveling?

    (해외에서 휴대폰을 주로 어떤 용도로 가장 많이 사용하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm looking for a prepaid SIM card.

    (선불 유심 카드를 찾고 있습니다.)

  • How much data is included in this plan?

    (이 요금제에는 데이터가 얼마나 포함되어 있나요?)

  • Could you help me activate it?

    (개통하는 것 좀 도와주시겠어요?)

  • That's a bit expensive for me.

    (저에게는 조금 비싸네요.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I mostly use Wi-Fi, so I don't need a big plan. (주로 와이파이를 써서 큰 요금제가 필요하지 않아요.)

  • Is there a plan with more data for around $30? (30달러 정도에 데이터가 더 많은 요금제가 있나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Prepaid plans are very popular for Working Holiday makers as they offer flexibility.

  • If a salesperson offers an expensive plan, it's totally okay to say "That's a bit expensive, do you have a cheaper option?"

Temporary Accommodation (임시 숙소: 문제 해결하기)

Today's Goal: Explain a problem clearly and request a solution politely.

Photo: A frustrated traveler looking at a broken heater or checking their phone in an Airbnb.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever stayed in a hostel or an Airbnb? What was your worst experience?

    (호스텔이나 에어비앤비에 머물러 본 적이 있나요? 가장 최악이었던 경험은 무엇인가요?)

  • What would you do if the room next door was too noisy at night?

    (밤에 옆방이 너무 시끄럽다면 어떻게 하시겠습니까?)

  • How do you stay polite when complaining in English?

    (영어로 항의/불만 제기를 할 때 어떻게 예의를 유지하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I have a reservation under the name ____.

    (____라는 이름으로 예약했습니다.)

  • There seems to be a problem with the Wi-Fi / heater.

    (와이파이 / 히터에 문제가 있는 것 같습니다.)

  • Could you take a look at it, please?

    (한번 봐 주시겠어요?)

  • Is it possible to change rooms?

    (방을 바꾸는 것이 가능할까요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • The hot water hasn't been working since last night. (어젯밤부터 뜨거운 물이 안 나옵니다.)

  • Could you please arrange a room change if it can't be fixed today? (오늘 고칠 수 없다면 방 변경을 준비해 주시겠어요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Saying "There seems to be a problem with..." sounds much more polite and native than "The heater is broken."

  • Airbnb hosts appreciate a direct but polite message if you have an issue.

You
