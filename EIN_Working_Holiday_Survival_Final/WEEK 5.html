Calling in Sick (아파서 결근/병가 전화하기)

Today's Goal: Call your manager to say you are sick and cannot come to work.
(오늘의 목표: 아파서 출근할 수 없다고 매니저에게 전화로 알리기)

Photo: A person looking sick and calling their manager from bed.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever had to call in sick for work or school?

    (직장이나 학교에 아파서 못 간다고 연락해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Is it better to call or send a text message when you are sick?

    (아플 때는 전화를 하는 것이 좋을까요, 아니면 문자를 보내는 것이 좋을까요?)

  • Why do managers need to know as early as possible?

    (매니저에게 왜 최대한 빨리 알려야 할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm calling because I'm not feeling well.

    (몸이 좋지 않아서 전화드렸습니다.)

  • I don't think I can come to work today.

    (오늘 출근하지 못할 것 같습니다.)

  • I have a bad cold / fever / stomachache.

    (심한 감기 / 열 / 복통이 있습니다.)

  • I'm really sorry for the short notice.

    (갑작스럽게(너무 임박해서) 알려드려 정말 죄송합니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I will go to the doctor and get a medical certificate. (병원에 가서 진단서를 받아오겠습니다.)

  • I will keep you updated on how I feel tomorrow. (내일 상태가 어떤지 계속 업데이트해 드리겠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia and NZ, "Calling in sick" requires calling, not just texting. Texts can be ignored or missed.

  • If you are full-time or part-time, you might need a "medical certificate" (진단서) to get paid sick leave.

3. Model Dialogue (실전 대화 모델)

You
Hi Manager, this is Minho. Do you have a second?(매니저님 안녕하세요, 민호입니다. 잠깐 시간 되시나요?)
Manager
Hi Minho, sure. Is everything okay?(안녕 민호, 그럼. 다 괜찮은 거니?)
You
I'm calling because I'm not feeling well today. I have a bad fever.(오늘 몸이 안 좋아서 전화드렸어요. 열이 심하게 납니다.)
Manager
Oh, I'm sorry to hear that. Can you work your shift tomorrow?(오, 저런. 내일 교대 근무는 할 수 있겠니?)
You
I don't think I can come to work today or tomorrow. I'm really sorry for the short notice.(오늘도 내일도 출근하기 어려울 것 같아요. 갑자기 말씀드려 정말 죄송합니다.)
Manager
That's okay, get some rest. Let me know how you feel on Wednesday.(괜찮아, 푹 쉬어. 수요일에 상태가 어떤지 알려줘.)

4. Guided Practice (핵심 문장 연습)

  1. State the reason for calling:
    I'm calling because I'm _______________ .
  2. State clearly you can't work:
    I don't think I can _______________ .
  3. Explain what's wrong:
    I have a bad _______________ .
  4. Apologize professionally:
    I'm really sorry for the _______________ .

6. Talk More

  • Do you feel guilty when you have to call in sick?(병가를 내야 할 때 죄책감을 느끼시나요?)

  • What happens if you don't call and just don't show up to work?(전화도 안 하고 무단결근(no-show)하면 어떻게 될까요?)

Asking for a Day Off (휴가 및 데이오프 요청하기)

Today's Goal: Politely ask your manager for time off in advance.
(오늘의 목표: 매니저에게 사전에 정중하게 휴가를 요청하기)

Photo: An employee talking to their manager while pointing at a calendar.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How far in advance should you ask for a day off?

    (휴가는 얼마나 미리 요청해야 할까요?)

  • Do you find it difficult to ask your boss for a vacation?

    (상사에게 휴가를 요청하는 것이 어렵게 느껴지나요?)

  • Why might a manager say "no" to your request?

    (매니저가 요청을 거절하는 이유는 무엇일까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I was wondering if I could take ___ off.

    (제가 ___에 쉴 수 있을지 궁금합니다.)

  • Is it possible to get next Friday off?

    (다음 주 금요일에 쉬는 것이 가능할까요?)

  • I need to take some time off for personal reasons.

    (개인적인 사정으로 휴가를 좀 내야 합니다.)

  • I can work extra shifts to make up for it.

    (그 시간(휴가)을 보충하기 위해 추가 근무를 할 수 있습니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I wanted to put in a leave request for next month. (다음 달에 휴가 신청을 하고 싶습니다.)

  • I will make sure my tasks are covered before I leave. (제가 떠나기 전에 업무가 차질 없도록 확실히 해두겠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Most managers require 2 weeks' notice for time off. Don't ask for a day off 2 days before!

  • "Make up for it" (보충하다) is a great phrase to show you are a responsible employee.

3. Model Dialogue (실전 대화 모델)

You
Hi Manager, do you have a minute to talk about the schedule?(매니저님, 스케줄 관련해서 잠깐 이야기할 수 있을까요?)
Manager
Sure, what's up?(그럼, 무슨 일이야?)
You
I was wondering if I could take next Friday off. I need to handle some personal matters.(다음 주 금요일에 쉴 수 있을까 해서요. 개인적인 일을 좀 처리해야 해서요.)
Manager
Next Friday? Let me check the roster. It might be busy.(다음 주 금요일? 근무표를 한번 볼게. 좀 바쁠 것 같은데.)
You
I can work an extra shift on Sunday to make up for it if that helps.(도움이 된다면 일요일에 보충(추가) 근무를 할 수 있습니다.)
Manager
That would be very helpful. Yes, you can take Friday off.(그렇게 해주면 큰 도움이 되겠네. 그래, 금요일에 쉬어.)

4. Guided Practice (핵심 문장 연습)

  1. Polite request:
    I was wondering if I could take _______________ off.
  2. Alternative direct request:
    Is it possible to get _______________ off?
  3. State the reason safely:
    I need to take time off for _______________ .
  4. Offer a solution:
    I can work extra shifts to _______________ .

LESSON 22: Asking for a Day Off - Wrap-up & Assignment

6. Talk More

  • Why is "I was wondering if..." better than "I want..."?("I want"보다 "I was wondering if"가 더 나은 표현인 이유는 무엇일까요?)

  • How do you feel when your coworker takes a day off and you have to work harder?(동료가 쉬어서 당신이 더 힘들게 일해야 할 때 기분이 어떠신가요?)

  • If your manager says NO, what would you do?(만약 매니저가 안 된다고(거절) 하면 어떻게 하실 건가요?)

Tutor Tip

• Emphasize the importance of "I was wondering if I could..." It is the softest, most polite way to ask a superior for a favor.

• "Personal reasons" is a great way to keep things private without lying.

Example:

Understanding Payslips (급여 명세서 이해 및 문제 제기)

Today's Goal: Talk about your payslip and politely ask if there is a mistake in your pay.
(오늘의 목표: 급여 명세서에 대해 이야기하고 급여에 오류가 있을 때 정중하게 묻기)

Photo: A person calculating finances on a computer while checking a document.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually check your bank account or payslip carefully?

    (평소 통장이나 급여 명세서를 꼼꼼히 확인하시나요?)

  • What would you do if your company paid you less than you worked?

    (일한 것보다 월급이 적게 들어왔다면 어떻게 하시겠습니까?)

  • Why is it hard to talk about money with your boss?

    (상사와 돈(급여)에 대해 이야기하는 것이 어려운 이유는 무엇일까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I was looking at my payslip and I have a question.

    (급여 명세서를 보다가 질문이 생겼습니다.)

  • I think there might be a mistake with my hours.

    (제 근무 시간에 착오가 있는 것 같습니다.)

  • I worked 30 hours, but I was only paid for 25.

    (저는 30시간을 일했는데 25시간에 대한 급여만 받았습니다.)

  • Could you please check this for me?

    (이 부분 한 번 확인해 주실 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I haven't received my pay for this week yet. (아직 이번 주 급여를 받지 못했습니다.)

  • Can you explain how the tax was calculated? (세금이 어떻게 계산된 것인지 설명해 주실 수 있나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia and NZ, pay is usually weekly or fortnightly (every 2 weeks).

  • "Payslip" is the document showing your gross pay (before tax) and net pay (after tax). Always keep them safe!

Asking for Help at Work (직장에서 도움 요청하기)

Today's Goal: Politely ask coworkers or your manager for help or instructions.
(오늘의 목표: 동료나 매니저에게 정중하게 도움이나 지시 사항 물어보기)

Photo: A new employee asking a colleague a question at their desk.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Are you afraid to ask questions when you don't know something?

    (모르는 것이 있을 때 질문하는 것이 두려우신가요?)

  • What happens if you guess how to do a task and get it wrong?

    (어떻게 하는지 짐작해서 일했다가 틀리게 되면 어떤 일이 벌어질까요?)

  • Why do employers prefer employees who ask questions?

    (고용주들은 왜 질문하는 직원을 선호할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, could you help me with this?

    (실례합니다, 이것 좀 도와주시겠어요?)

  • I'm not sure how to do this.

    (이걸 어떻게 해야 할지 잘 모르겠습니다.)

  • Could you show me how to use the ___?

    (___ 사용법을 보여주시겠어요?)

  • Could you please explain that one more time?

    (한 번 더 설명해 주실 수 있나요?)

  • What should I do next?

    (다음엔 제가 무엇을 해야 할까요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I want to make sure I am doing this correctly. (제가 이것을 맞게 하고 있는지 확실히 하고 싶습니다.)

  • Sorry to bother you, but I have a quick question. (방해해서 죄송하지만, 간단한 질문 하나 있습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Western work culture, asking questions is a sign of intelligence and care, not incompetence. Never pretend to understand if you don't!

  • "Sorry to bother you" is a great polite opener when interrupting a busy coworker.

EIN WORKING HOLIDAY SURVIVAL WEEK 5 • LESSON 25

Apologizing for Mistakes (실수에 대해 사과하고 수습하기)

Today's Goal: Apologize professionally when you break something or make a mistake.
(오늘의 목표: 무언가를 깨뜨리거나 실수했을 때 프로페셔널하게 사과하기)

Photo: A worker dropping a glass or spilling coffee, looking shocked.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever broken a dish or spilled something at work?

    (직장에서 접시를 깨거나 무언가를 엎지른 적이 있나요?)

  • What is the first thing you should do when you make a mistake?

    (실수했을 때 가장 먼저 해야 할 일은 무엇인가요?)

  • Is saying "I'm sorry" enough, or do you need to do something else?

    ("죄송합니다"라고 말하는 것만으로 충분한가요, 아니면 다른 조치가 더 필요한가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm so sorry. I made a mistake.

    (정말 죄송합니다. 제가 실수를 했습니다.)

  • I accidentally dropped/spilled ___.

    (실수로 ___을(를) 떨어뜨렸습니다/엎질렀습니다.)

  • I'll clean it up right away.

    (제가 즉시 치우겠습니다.)

  • I will be more careful next time.

    (다음부터는 더 조심하겠습니다.)

  • How can I fix this?

    (이걸 어떻게 수습할 수 있을까요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • It was my fault completely. (전적으로 제 잘못입니다.)

  • I'll make a new one immediately. (당장 새것으로 다시 만들겠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Mistakes happen in cafes and restaurants every day (dropping a plate, entering the wrong order). Own up to it fast!

  • Don't say "It wasn't my fault." Instead, say "I made a mistake, how can I fix it?" Managers respect accountability.

LESSON 25: Apologizing for Mistakes - Practice & Role-play

3. Model Dialogue (실전 대화 모델)

You
Manager, I'm so sorry. I made a mistake.(매니저님, 정말 죄송합니다. 제가 실수를 했어요.)
Manager
What happened?(무슨 일이니?)
You
I accidentally dropped a tray of glasses. They broke on the floor.(실수로 유리잔이 든 쟁반을 떨어뜨렸습니다. 바닥에서 깨졌어요.)
Manager
Are you hurt? Is anyone hurt?(너 다친 데는 없고? 다친 사람 있니?)
You
No, everyone is fine. I'll clean it up right away.(아니요, 아무도 안 다쳤습니다. 제가 즉시 치우겠습니다.)
Manager
Okay, grab the broom. Be careful with the glass.(알았어, 빗자루 가져와. 유리조심하고.)
You
I will. I will be more careful next time.(네. 다음부터는 더 조심하겠습니다.)

4. Guided Practice (핵심 문장 연습)

  1. Own the mistake:
    I'm so sorry. I ______________________ .
  2. Explain what happened:
    I accidentally dropped / spilled ______________________ .
  3. Take action:
    I'll ______________________ right away.
  4. Ask how to solve it:
    How can I ______________________ ?
  5. Reassure the manager:
    I will be ______________________ next time.
