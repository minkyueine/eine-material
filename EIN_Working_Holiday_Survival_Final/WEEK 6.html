Arranging a Room Inspection (방 인스펙션 잡기)

Today's Goal: Call or message a landlord to arrange a time to see a room.
(오늘의 목표: 집주인에게 연락해 방을 보러 갈 시간(인스펙션) 예약하기)

Photo: A person inspecting a clean, sunny bedroom before moving in.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever lived in a share house before?

    (전에 쉐어하우스에 살아본 적이 있나요?)

  • What is the most important thing when choosing a room?

    (방을 고를 때 가장 중요한 것은 무엇인가요?)

  • Why is it important to see the room in person?

    (직접 가서 방을 보는 것이 왜 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm calling about the room for rent.

    (임대 내놓으신 방 때문에 연락드렸습니다.)

  • Is the room still available?

    (그 방 아직 남아 있나요?)

  • Can I come for an inspection?

    (제가 방을 보러(인스펙션) 가도 될까요?)

  • What time suits you best?

    (언제가 가장 편하신가요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I saw your ad on Facebook/Gumtree. (페이스북/검트리에서 구인 광고를 보았습니다.)

  • I am looking to move in as soon as possible. (가능한 한 빨리 입주하고 싶습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia and NZ, checking a room is called an "Inspection." Never sign a contract or pay money before doing an inspection!

  • It is very common to text the landlord first before calling.

Asking About Rent and Bond (방세 및 보증금 묻기)

Today's Goal: Ask crucial questions about weekly rent, bills, and the security deposit.
(오늘의 목표: 주세, 공과금, 그리고 보증금에 관한 중요한 질문하기)

Photo: A person calculating finances or handing over a rental agreement.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • In Korea, do people usually pay rent monthly or weekly?

    (한국에서는 보통 월세와 주세 중 어떤 것으로 내나요?)

  • What are 'utility bills' (gas, water, electricity)?

    ('공과금(가스, 수도, 전기)'이란 무엇인가요?)

  • Why do landlords ask for a security deposit?

    (집주인들은 왜 보증금을 요구할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • How much is the rent per week?

    (주당 방세는 얼마인가요?)

  • Are all bills included?

    (모든 공과금이 포함되어 있나요?)

  • How much is the bond?

    (보증금(본드비)은 얼마인가요?)

  • When is the rent due?

    (방세는 언제 내야 하나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Is there a minimum stay requirement? (최소 체류 기간 조건이 있나요?)

  • Do I need to give two weeks' notice before moving out? (이사가기 전에 2주 전 노티스(알림)를 줘야 하나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • "Bond" is the Australian/NZ term for "Security Deposit". It is usually equal to 2 to 4 weeks of rent.

  • In most share houses, rent is paid weekly or fortnightly (every 2 weeks), unlike the monthly system in Korea.

Discussing House Rules (쉐어하우스 규칙 논의하기)

Today's Goal: Talk about chores, visitors, and rules with your flatmates.
(오늘의 목표: 플랫메이트(룸메이트)와 청소, 방문객, 규칙에 대해 대화하기)

Photo: Flatmates hanging out in a shared kitchen or cleaning up together.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever lived with people other than your family?

    (가족이 아닌 다른 사람들과 살아본 적이 있나요?)

  • What is the most annoying habit a flatmate can have?

    (플랫메이트(룸메이트)가 가질 수 있는 가장 짜증 나는 습관은 무엇일까요?)

  • Why are house rules important in a share house?

    (쉐어하우스에서 하우스 룰이 왜 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • What are the house rules here?

    (이곳의 하우스 룰은 무엇인가요?)

  • We take turns cleaning the bathroom.

    (우리는 화장실 청소를 교대로 합니다.)

  • Please clean up after yourself.

    (본인이 쓴 것은 스스로 치워주세요.)

  • Are friends allowed to stay over?

    (친구가 자고 가도 되나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • We usually split the cost of toilet paper and dish soap. (보통 휴지와 주방세제 비용은 나눠서 냅니다.)

  • Please keep the noise down after 10 PM. (밤 10시 이후에는 조용히 해주세요.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • "Flatmate" (UK/Aus/NZ) or "Roommate" (US/Canada). In Australia, roommate usually means someone you share the *same bedroom* with.

  • Cleaning rosters (청소 당번표) are very common. Always respect communal spaces (kitchen/living room).

Buying Second-hand Furniture (중고 가구 거래하기)

Today's Goal: Message a seller online to ask about an item, negotiate, and arrange pick-up.
(오늘의 목표: 판매자에게 연락해 물건 상태를 묻고, 흥정하며, 픽업 시간 정하기)

Photo: A nice second-hand sofa or desk being viewed by a buyer.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever used an app like Karrot Market (당근마켓) in Korea?

    (한국에서 당근마켓 같은 중고 거래 앱을 사용해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • What kind of furniture do you usually need when moving into an unfurnished room?

    (가구가 없는 방에 입주할 때 주로 어떤 가구가 필요한가요?)

  • Are you good at negotiating prices?

    (가격 흥정(네고)을 잘하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Hi, is this still available?

    (안녕하세요, 이거 아직 판매 중인가요?)

  • What is the condition of the desk/bed?

    (책상/침대 상태가 어떤가요?)

  • Would you take $50 for it?

    (50달러에 주실 수 있을까요?)

  • Can I pick it up today?

    (오늘 픽업(수령)하러 가도 될까요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Are there any scratches or damage? (스크래치나 파손된 부분이 있나요?)

  • Do you offer delivery for an extra fee? (추가 비용을 내면 배달도 해주시나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Facebook Marketplace and Gumtree (in Aus/UK) are the most popular places to buy cheap second-hand stuff.

  • "OBO" on an ad means "Or Best Offer" (가격 제안 가능), so you can always try to negotiate politely!

Reporting Maintenance Issues (집 수리 및 문제 요청하기)

Today's Goal: Tell your landlord about a broken appliance or leak in your house.
(오늘의 목표: 집주인에게 가전제품 고장이나 누수 등에 대해 알리고 수리 요청하기)

Photo: A person looking under a leaking kitchen sink with a worried face.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What is the most annoying thing to break in a house? (Toilet, Wi-Fi, AC?)

    (집에서 고장 났을 때 가장 짜증 나는 것은 무엇인가요? 변기, 와이파이, 에어컨?)

  • Have you ever had a water leak or a power outage at home?

    (집에 물이 새거나 정전이 일어난 적이 있나요?)

  • Why is it important to tell the landlord immediately?

    (집주인에게 즉시 알리는 것이 왜 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Hi, I'm calling to report a problem.

    (안녕하세요, 문제를 보고하려고 연락드렸습니다.)

  • The washing machine is broken.

    (세탁기가 고장 났습니다.)

  • The sink is leaking.

    (싱크대에서 물이 샙니다.)

  • When can you send someone to fix it?

    (언제 고칠 사람을 보내주실 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • The toilet is blocked. (변기가 막혔습니다.)

  • We have no hot water in the shower. (샤워기에서 뜨거운 물이 안 나옵니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In most rental agreements, if an appliance provided by the landlord breaks (and it's not your fault), the landlord MUST pay for the repair.

  • Take a photo or video of the problem and text it to your landlord or property manager right away.
