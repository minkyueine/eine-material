Seeing a Doctor (병원 진료 받기)

Today's Goal: Explain your symptoms to a doctor and ask for a medical certificate.
(오늘의 목표: 의사에게 증상을 설명하고 병가 진단서 요청하기)

Photo: A patient talking to a doctor in a clinic setting.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever been sick in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 아파본 적이 있나요?)

  • Why is it important to have travel insurance?

    (여행자 보험을 갖는 것이 왜 중요할까요?)

  • Do you usually go to the doctor immediately when you catch a cold?

    (감기에 걸렸을 때 주로 바로 병원에 가시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to see a doctor, please.

    (의사 선생님 진료를 보고 싶습니다.)

  • I have been feeling dizzy / sick since yesterday.

    (어제부터 어지럽고 / 아픕니다.)

  • My throat hurts, and I have a fever.

    (목이 아프고 열이 납니다.)

  • I need a medical certificate for work.

    (직장 제출용 진단서가 필요합니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • How much is the consultation fee? (진료비가 얼마인가요?)

  • Can you write a prescription for me? (처방전을 써 주실 수 있나요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia/NZ, local clinics are called "GP" (General Practitioner). You must book an appointment to see a GP.

  • A "medical certificate" is legally required by employers to grant paid sick leave. Don't forget to ask for it!

Reporting a Stolen Item (도난 신고하기)

Today's Goal: Report a stolen phone or wallet at the police station.
(오늘의 목표: 경찰서에서 휴대폰이나 지갑 도난을 신고하기)

Photo: A traveler speaking anxiously to a police officer at a desk.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever lost something important while traveling?

    (여행 중 중요한 물건을 잃어버린 적이 있나요?)

  • What is the first thing you should do if your wallet is stolen?

    (지갑을 도난당했다면 가장 먼저 해야 할 일은 무엇인가요?)

  • Why do you need to go to the police station for a stolen item?

    (물건을 도난당했을 때 왜 경찰서에 가야 할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to report a stolen wallet / phone.

    (지갑/휴대폰 도난 신고를 하고 싶습니다.)

  • It happened at the train station / a cafe.

    (기차역/카페에서 일어난 일입니다.)

  • My wallet contained my ID and credit cards.

    (제 지갑에는 신분증과 신용카드들이 들어 있었습니다.)

  • I need a police report for my insurance.

    (보험 처리를 위해 경찰 신고 접수증(폴리스 리포트)이 필요합니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I already canceled my credit cards. (신용카드는 이미 정지시켰습니다.)

  • I don't remember exactly when it was taken. (정확히 언제 도난당했는지 기억나지 않습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • To claim travel insurance, you absolutely MUST have a "Police Report" from the local station. Always ask for a copy.

  • Pickpocketing (소매치기) is very common in major European cities and busy transit hubs.

Giving Notice at Work (퇴사 통보하기)

Today's Goal: Politely tell your manager you are leaving the job and state your last day.
(오늘의 목표: 매니저에게 예의 바르게 퇴사를 알리고 마지막 근무일 정하기)

Photo: An employee having a serious but polite conversation with their manager.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever quit a part-time job or a full-time job?

    (아르바이트나 정규직을 그만둔 적이 있나요?)

  • How far in advance should you tell your boss you are leaving?

    (상사에게 퇴사 사실을 얼마나 미리 알려야 할까요?)

  • Why is it important to leave a job on good terms?

    (직장을 좋은 관계로 마무리하며 떠나는 것이 왜 중요할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I need to give my two weeks' notice.

    (2주 전 퇴사 통보(노티스)를 드려야 할 것 같습니다.)

  • My last day will be ___.

    (제 마지막 근무일은 ___가 될 것입니다.)

  • I am moving to another city.

    (다른 도시로 이사를 가게 되었습니다.)

  • I have really enjoyed working here.

    (이곳에서 일하는 동안 정말 즐거웠습니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Thank you for all the opportunities. (그동안 주신 모든 기회에 감사드립니다.)

  • I will make sure my work is handed over properly. (업무 인수인계가 제대로 되도록 확실히 하겠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In most Western workplaces, "Two weeks' notice" is the standard polite rule before quitting.

  • Even if you hated the job, always say "I enjoyed working here." You might need them as a reference later!

Requesting a Reference (추천서/레퍼런스 부탁하기)

Today's Goal: Ask your manager to be a reference for your next job application.
(오늘의 목표: 다음 구직을 위해 매니저에게 추천인(레퍼런스)이 되어달라고 부탁하기)

Photo: A happy employee shaking hands with a supportive manager.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What is a 'reference check' in Western work culture?

    (서양 직장 문화에서 '레퍼런스 체크(평판 조회)'란 무엇인가요?)

  • Have you ever asked an old boss to say good things about you?

    (예전 상사에게 당신에 대해 좋은 말을 해달라고 부탁해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Why do new employers want to call your old employers?

    (새로운 고용주는 왜 예전 고용주에게 전화를 걸고 싶어 할까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Would you be comfortable acting as my reference?

    (제 추천인(레퍼런스)이 되어 주실 수 있나요?)

  • I'm applying for a new job.

    (새로운 직장에 지원하고 있습니다.)

  • Can I give them your phone number?

    (그들에게 매니저님의 전화번호를 드려도 될까요?)

  • Thank you for your support.

    (지원/지지해 주셔서 감사합니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • They might call you next week. (그들이 다음 주에 전화할 수도 있습니다.)

  • I learned so much from working with you. (매니저님과 일하며 정말 많은 것을 배웠습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia, NZ, and Canada, "Reference Checks" are mandatory for almost all jobs. Always ask your manager before giving out their number!

  • What happens if you give a reference without asking them first?(사전 허락 없이 추천인의 연락처를 넘기면 어떤 일이 발생할까요?)

  • What good things do you think your current boss would say about you?(현재 상사가 당신에 대해 어떤 좋은 말을 해줄 것 같나요?)

Tutor Tip

• Emphasize the phrasing "Would you be comfortable..." It gives the manager a polite way to say yes or no.

• A "reference check" is a phone call. A "reference letter" is a written document.
