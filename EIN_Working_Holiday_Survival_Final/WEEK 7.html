Grocery Shopping (슈퍼마켓에서 장보기)

Today's Goal: Use self-checkout and handle common supermarket situations.
(오늘의 목표: 셀프 계산대를 이용하고 마트에서 흔히 발생하는 상황 대처하기)

Photo: A person scanning items at a self-checkout machine in a supermarket.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How often do you go grocery shopping?

    (얼마나 자주 장을 보러 가시나요?)

  • Do you usually use self-checkout or go to the cashier?

    (주로 셀프 계산대를 이용하시나요, 아니면 캐셔에게 가시나요?)

  • What happens if an item doesn't scan at the self-checkout?

    (셀프 계산대에서 물건 바코드가 안 찍히면 어떻게 하나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, where can I find the ___?

    (실례합니다, ___은(는) 어디 있나요?)

  • I need a plastic bag, please.

    (비닐봉지 하나 주세요.)

  • The machine is not taking my card.

    (기계가 제 카드를 인식하지 않아요.)

  • This item won't scan.

    (이 물건 바코드가 안 찍혀요.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Could you help me clear this error? (이 오류 푸는 것 좀 도와주시겠어요?)

  • I brought my own bags. (장바구니를 챙겨왔습니다.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia (Coles/Woolworths) and the UK (Tesco/Sainsbury's), almost everyone uses self-checkout. If you make a mistake, a light turns red and staff will come to assist you.

  • Plastic bags cost extra almost everywhere now. Say "I brought my own bags" to save money.

At the Post Office (우체국에서 소포 보내기)

Today's Goal: Send a parcel internationally and ask about shipping options.
(오늘의 목표: 해외로 소포를 보내고 배송 옵션에 대해 묻기)

Photo: A person handing a cardboard box to a clerk at a post office counter.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever sent a package to another country?

    (다른 나라로 소포를 보내본 적이 있나요?)

  • What items do people usually send back home?

    (사람들은 주로 어떤 물건을 고향(본국)으로 보내나요?)

  • Do you prefer standard shipping or express shipping?

    (일반 배송과 특급 배송 중 어떤 것을 선호하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to send this parcel to South Korea.

    (이 소포를 한국으로 보내고 싶습니다.)

  • What is inside the box?

    (상자 안에 무엇이 들어 있나요?)

  • It just contains clothes and some snacks.

    (옷과 과자 약간이 들어 있습니다.)

  • How long will it take by standard shipping?

    (일반 배송으로 하면 얼마나 걸리나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Does it include a tracking number? (배송 조회(트래킹) 번호가 포함되나요?)

  • I need to buy a padded envelope. (뽁뽁이 봉투를 하나 사야 해요.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • "Parcel" is mainly used in the UK/Aus/NZ. In the US/Canada, "Package" is more common.

  • You must fill out a customs declaration form (세관 신고서). Never send prohibited items like meat or fresh fruit!

At the Pharmacy or Clinic (약국 또는 병원에서)

Today's Goal: Explain your symptoms clearly and buy medicine or see a doctor.
(오늘의 목표: 증상을 명확하게 설명하고 약을 사거나 의사 진료 받기)

Photo: A patient talking to a doctor or pharmacist.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever been sick while living abroad?

    (해외에 사는 동안 아파본 적이 있나요?)

  • Why is it scary to go to the hospital in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 병원에 가는 것이 무서운 이유는 무엇일까요?)

  • What kind of medicine do you usually bring from Korea?

    (한국에서 올 때 주로 어떤 상비약을 챙겨 오시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I have a bad cough / stomachache.

    (기침 / 복통이 심합니다.)

  • Do you have anything for a cold / a headache?

    (감기 / 두통에 먹을 약이 있나요?)

  • I'd like to see a doctor, please.

    (의사 선생님 진료를 보고 싶습니다.)

  • I need a medical certificate for work.

    (직장에 제출할 진단서가 필요합니다.)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I have travel insurance. (저는 여행자 보험이 있습니다.)

  • Does this medicine make you drowsy? (이 약을 먹으면 졸린가요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • In Australia/NZ, local clinics are called "GP" (General Practitioner). You must see a GP first before going to a hospital, unless it's an emergency.

  • To get paid sick leave at work, your manager will usually ask for a "Medical Certificate" from the GP or Pharmacy.

Going to the Hair Salon (미용실 가기)

Today's Goal: Tell a hairdresser how you want your hair cut or styled.
(오늘의 목표: 미용사에게 자신이 원하는 헤어스타일과 컷을 설명하기)

Photo: A person getting a haircut in a bright, modern salon.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How often do you get a haircut?

    (머리를 얼마나 자주 자르시나요?)

  • Are you nervous about getting your hair cut in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 머리 자르는 것이 긴장되시나요?)

  • Do you usually show a photo to the hairdresser?

    (미용사에게 주로 사진을 보여주시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like a haircut, please.

    (머리 자르고 싶습니다.)

  • Just a trim, please.

    (조금만 다듬어 주세요.)

  • Not too short, please.

    (너무 짧지 않게 해주세요.)

  • Could you make it like this photo?

    (이 사진처럼 해 주실 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • Could you thin it out a little? (숱을 조금 쳐주시겠어요?)

  • I want to dye my hair dark brown. (머리를 어두운 갈색으로 염색하고 싶어요.)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • Showing a picture on your phone is the easiest and safest way to avoid a bad haircut abroad.

  • "Just a trim" means cutting off a very small amount (about 1-2 cm) to keep the shape.

    (직장 동료들과 친구가 되는 것이 쉬운가요?)

  • What app do people use to chat in your country?

    (당신의 나라에서는 사람들이 주로 어떤 채팅 앱을 사용하나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Do you want to grab a coffee/drink after work?

    (일 끝나고 커피/술 한잔할래요?)

  • What are you doing on your day off?

    (쉬는 날에 뭐 하세요?)

  • Are you on Instagram?

    (인스타그램 하시나요?)

  • Let's keep in touch!

    (계속 연락하고 지내요!)

Upgrade (Professional)

  • I'd love to hang out sometime. (언제 한 번 놀고 싶어요.)

  • Can I get your number? (전화번호 좀 받을 수 있을까요?)

Real-life Working Holiday Tip

  • "Hang out" means to spend free time together. It is very casual and commonly used.

  • In Western countries, asking for someone's Instagram is often more casual and less pressured than asking for their phone number.
