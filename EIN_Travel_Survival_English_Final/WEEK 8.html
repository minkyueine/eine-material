Lost Luggage (수하물 분실 대처하기)

Today's Goal: Report lost luggage at the airport and ask for help.
(오늘의 목표: 공항에서 분실한 수하물을 신고하고 도움 요청하기)

Photo: A frustrated traveler looking for bags at the baggage claim area.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever lost your luggage on a trip?

    (여행 중 수하물을 잃어버린 적이 있나요?)

  • What should you do if your bag doesn't come out?

    (가방이 나오지 않으면 어떻게 해야 할까요?)

  • Do you pack essential items in your carry-on bag?

    (필수품은 기내 수하물(기내용 가방)에 챙기는 편인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, my luggage hasn't arrived.

    (실례합니다, 제 수하물이 도착하지 않았습니다.)

  • Here is my baggage claim tag.

    (여기 제 수하물 보관증(태그)입니다.)

  • My bag is ___ and ___.

    (제 가방은 [색상]이고 [크기/특징]입니다.)

  • When will it be delivered?

    (언제 배송되나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • It has a name tag on it. (이름표가 달려 있습니다.)

  • Could you check the system, please? (시스템을 한 번 확인해 주시겠어요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Always keep your baggage claim tags (given at check-in). They are essential to track lost bags.

  • Most airlines will find your bag and deliver it to your hotel within 24-48 hours for free.

Feeling Sick at a Pharmacy (약국에서 증상 말하기)

Today's Goal: Explain your symptoms to a pharmacist and buy medicine.
(오늘의 목표: 약사에게 증상을 설명하고 약 구매하기)

Photo: A customer talking to a pharmacist over the counter in a modern pharmacy.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever been sick while traveling?

    (여행 중에 아팠던 적이 있나요?)

  • Do you bring medicine from home when you travel?

    (여행 갈 때 집에서 상비약을 챙겨 가시나요?)

  • What are common sicknesses people get while traveling?

    (사람들이 여행 중에 흔히 겪는 질병은 무엇일까요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm not feeling well.

    (몸이 별로 안 좋아요.)

  • I have a headache / stomachache / fever.

    (두통 / 복통 / 열이 있습니다.)

  • Do you have anything for a cold?

    (감기에 먹을 만한 약이 있나요?)

  • How often should I take this?

    (이 약을 얼마나 자주 먹어야 하나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Does this make you sleepy? (이 약을 먹으면 졸린가요?)

  • I need some band-aids. (반창고(밴드)가 필요해요.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In many European countries, you can talk directly to a pharmacist for minor illnesses instead of seeing a doctor.

  • Note: It's called a "Pharmacy" or "Drugstore" in the US, and a "Chemist" in the UK.

Getting Lost (길을 잃었을 때)

Today's Goal: Explain that you are lost and ask for the way back to your hotel or a landmark.
(오늘의 목표: 길을 잃었다고 설명하고 호텔이나 랜드마크로 돌아가는 길 묻기)

Photo: A traveler looking confused while checking a map on a street corner.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you use Google Maps when you travel?

    (여행할 때 구글 지도를 사용하시나요?)

  • Do you easily panic when you get lost?

    (길을 잃으면 쉽게 당황하는(패닉에 빠지는) 편인가요?)

  • What do you do if your phone battery dies in a new city?

    (처음 가는 도시에서 휴대폰 배터리가 꺼지면 어떻게 하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, I think I'm lost.

    (실례합니다, 길을 잃은 것 같아요.)

  • Could you help me find ___?

    (___ 찾는 것 좀 도와주시겠어요?)

  • Can you point it out on the map?

    (지도에서 짚어주실 수 있나요?)

  • I need to get back to the city center.

    (시내 중심으로 돌아가야 해요.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • My phone battery is dead. (제 핸드폰 배터리가 나갔어요.)

  • Could you call a taxi for me? (택시 좀 불러 주시겠어요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Always take a business card from your hotel front desk. If your phone dies, you can just show the card to a taxi driver or local.

LESSON 38: Getting Lost - Wrap-up & Assignment

6. Talk More

  • What is the most difficult thing about finding your way in a new city?(새로운 도시에서 길을 찾을 때 가장 어려운 점은 무엇인가요?)

Emergency at the Hotel (호텔에서의 비상 상황)

Today's Goal: Report an emergency or urgent problem to the hotel staff.
(오늘의 목표: 호텔 직원에게 비상 상황이나 긴급한 문제를 알리기)

Photo: A person urgently calling the front desk from their hotel room.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What constitutes an emergency in a hotel?

    (호텔에서 어떤 상황이 '비상 상황'이 될 수 있을까요?)

  • Who should you call first if something goes wrong?

    (문제가 생겼을 때 가장 먼저 누구에게 전화해야 할까요?)

  • Have you ever lost something important like a passport?

    (여권 같은 중요한 물건을 잃어버린 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I have an emergency.

    (비상 상황입니다.)

  • I lost my room key / passport.

    (방 열쇠 / 여권을 잃어버렸습니다.)

  • Somebody stole my bag.

    (누군가 제 가방을 훔쳐갔습니다.)

  • Could you call the police / an ambulance?

    (경찰 / 구급차를 불러주시겠어요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Please hurry. (서둘러 주세요.)

  • Is there a hospital nearby? (근처에 병원이 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In an emergency, always call the hotel front desk first. They speak the local language and can call authorities for you quickly.

Free Talk & Travel Review (프리 토크 및 여행 회고)

Today's Goal: Tell a short travel story and reflect on the Travel Survival course.
(오늘의 목표: 짧은 여행 이야기를 들려주고 40강의 코스 마무리하기)

Photo: A happy traveler waving or looking out over a beautiful landscape.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • You finished the course! How do you feel?

    (과정을 모두 마쳤습니다! 기분이 어떠신가요?)

  • Do you feel ready to travel using English?

    (이제 영어를 사용해 여행할 준비가 된 것 같나요?)

  • What was the most useful lesson for you?

    (당신에게 가장 유용했던 레슨은 무엇이었나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I went to ___.

    (저는 ___에 갔습니다.)

  • My favorite part was ___.

    (제가 가장 좋아했던 부분은 ___이었습니다.)

  • The food was ___.

    (음식은 ___했습니다.)

  • I would like to go again.

    (다시 가보고 싶습니다.)

  • Next time, I want to ___.

    (다음번에는 ___하고 싶습니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • It was an unforgettable trip. (정말 잊을 수 없는 여행이었습니다.)

LESSON 40: Travel Review - Wrap-up & Assignment

6. Talk More

  • Do you travel for food, nature, or culture?(음식, 자연, 문화 중 어떤 것을 위해 여행을 떠나시나요?)

  • If you could travel tomorrow, where would you go?(만약 내일 바로 여행을 갈 수 있다면, 어디로 가고 싶나요?)

  • What is your travel goal for this year?(올해 당신의 여행 목표는 무엇인가요?)

  • What kind of traveler are you now compared to before taking this class?(이 수업을 듣기 전과 비교했을 때, 지금 당신은 어떤 여행자가 되었나요?)

Tutor Tip

• Let the student talk first; don't interrupt too much.

• Ask follow-up questions to extend free talking.

• Congratulate the student highly on finishing all 40 lessons!
