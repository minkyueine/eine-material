Greeting and Small Talk (인사와 스몰 토크)

Today's Goal: Greet locals naturally and make simple small talk.
(오늘의 목표: 현지인들과 자연스럽게 인사하고 가벼운 스몰 토크 하기)

Photo: Two people smiling and chatting warmly over coffee.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually say hello to shop staff or strangers?

    (상점 직원이나 낯선 사람에게 주로 인사를 건네시나요?)

  • What is a safe topic for small talk?

    (스몰 토크를 위한 안전한 대화 주제는 무엇일까요?)

  • How do you answer when someone says "How are you?"

    (누군가 "How are you?"라고 물을 때 어떻게 대답하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Hi, how are you doing today?

    (안녕하세요, 오늘 기분이 어떠신가요?)

  • I'm good, thanks. And you?

    (저는 잘 지냅니다. 당신은요?)

  • Beautiful weather today, isn't it?

    (오늘 날씨 정말 좋네요, 그렇지 않나요?)

  • I'm visiting from Korea.

    (저는 한국에서 여행 왔습니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • It's my first time in this city. (이 도시는 처음 방문합니다.)

  • Are you from around here? (이 근처에 사시나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In the US/UK/Australia, it's very common for cashiers to ask "How are you?" Just say "Good, thanks!" and smile.

  • Weather is the most universal and polite small talk topic.

Talking About Your Trip (여행 일정과 경험 나누기)

Today's Goal: Share your travel plans and talk about what you have done so far.
(오늘의 목표: 여행 계획을 공유하고 지금까지 했던 경험들에 대해 이야기하기)

Photo: Travelers looking at a map and pointing at places they want to visit.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually plan every day of your trip?

    (여행할 때 매일의 일정을 다 계획하는 편인가요?)

  • What is your favorite thing to do on the first day?

    (여행 첫날에 가장 좋아하는 일은 무엇인가요?)

  • Do you easily make friends with other travelers?

    (다른 여행객들과 쉽게 친구가 되는 편인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I've been here for ___ days.

    (여기에 온 지 ___일 되었습니다.)

  • I'm planning to visit ___.

    (저는 ___에 방문할 계획입니다.)

  • I really enjoyed ___.

    (저는 ___이 정말 좋았습니다.)

  • How long are you staying?

    (얼마나 머무실 예정인가요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • I haven't been there yet. (거기는 아직 가보지 못했어요.)

  • This city is amazing. (이 도시는 정말 놀라워요/멋져요.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Meeting other travelers on tours is a great way to practice English.

  • "I've been here for [time]" is the most natural way to say how long your trip has been so far.

Asking for a Photo (사진 촬영 부탁하기)

Today's Goal: Politely ask a stranger to take a photo of you or your group.
(오늘의 목표: 낯선 사람에게 본인 또는 일행의 사진을 찍어달라고 정중하게 부탁하기)

Photo: A tourist handing their smartphone to a friendly stranger to take a photo.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you take a lot of photos when you travel?

    (여행할 때 사진을 많이 찍으시나요?)

  • Are you shy to ask strangers to take your photo?

    (낯선 사람에게 사진을 찍어달라고 부탁하는 것이 쑥스러우신가요?)

  • Has anyone ever asked you to take a photo for them?

    (누군가 당신에게 사진을 찍어달라고 부탁한 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, could you take a photo for us?

    (실례합니다, 저희 사진 좀 찍어주시겠어요?)

  • Just press this button.

    (이 버튼만 누르시면 됩니다.)

  • Could you include the background, please?

    (배경도 같이 나오게 찍어주시겠어요?)

  • Would you like me to take one for you?

    (제가 한 장 찍어드릴까요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Could you take one more, just in case? (혹시 모르니 한 장만 더 찍어주시겠어요?)

  • It looks great, thank you! (사진 잘 나왔네요, 감사합니다!)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • It's polite to offer to take a photo in return ("Would you like me to take one for you?").

  • Koreans are famous for taking great photos! Don't hesitate to ask other travelers.

Asking for Recommendations (현지 명소/맛집 추천받기)

Today's Goal: Ask hotel staff or locals for good places to eat or visit.
(오늘의 목표: 호텔 직원이나 현지인에게 맛집이나 명소 추천받기)

Photo: A traveler talking to a concierge at a desk, looking at a local guide map.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually search for restaurants online or ask locals?

    (주로 온라인으로 식당을 검색하나요, 아니면 현지인에게 묻나요?)

  • Have you ever found a hidden gem while traveling?

    (여행 중 숨겨진 명소(맛집)를 발견해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Who gives the best recommendations? (Friends, Google, Locals?)

    (누가 가장 좋은 추천을 해주나요? 친구, 구글, 현지인?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Do you have any recommendations for ___?

    (___에 대한 추천해 주실 곳이 있나요?)

  • Where do locals usually go?

    (현지인들은 주로 어디를 가나요?)

  • Is there a good cafe nearby?

    (근처에 좋은 카페가 있나요?)

  • What is your favorite place around here?

    (이 근처에서 당신이 가장 좋아하는 장소는 어디인가요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • I'm looking for something not too touristy. (관광객이 너무 많지 않은 곳을 찾고 있어요.)

  • Could you show me on the map? (지도에 표시해 주시겠어요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Asking hotel receptionists "Where do you personally eat?" often gets you better results than "Where is a good restaurant?"

  • "Not too touristy" is a great phrase to find authentic local experiences.

Saying Goodbye and Thank You (작별 인사와 감사의 표현)

Today's Goal: End a conversation politely and thank people for their help.
(오늘의 목표: 대화를 예의 바르게 마무리하고 도움에 대해 감사 표현하기)

Photo: Travelers waving goodbye or smiling as they leave a cafe.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How do you usually say goodbye to strangers?

    (낯선 사람과 헤어질 때 보통 어떻게 인사하시나요?)

  • Is it difficult to end a conversation in English?

    (영어로 대화를 끝맺는 것이 어렵게 느껴지나요?)

  • How do you express deep thanks to someone who helped you?

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Thank you so much for your help.

    (도와주셔서 정말 감사합니다.)

  • I really appreciate it.

    (정말 감사합니다 / 감사히 여기겠습니다.)

  • It was nice talking to you.

    (당신과 대화해서 즐거웠습니다.)

  • Have a great day!

    (좋은 하루 보내세요!)

  • Enjoy the rest of your trip.

    (남은 여행 즐겁게 보내세요.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Safe travels! (안전한 여행 되세요!)

  • I must be going now. (저는 이제 가봐야겠습니다.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • "I really appreciate it" sounds much more polite and sincere than just "Thank you".

  • Saying "Have a great day" as you leave a shop or finish speaking with someone is a staple of Western politeness.
