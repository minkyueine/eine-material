Reserving a Table (식당 예약하기)

Today's Goal: Make a restaurant reservation over the phone or in person.
(오늘의 목표: 전화나 직접 방문하여 식당 예약하기)

Photo: A nicely set dining table at an upscale restaurant.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually book restaurants in advance when traveling?

    (여행할 때 주로 식당을 미리 예약하시나요?)

  • What is your favorite type of restaurant?

    (당신이 가장 좋아하는 식당 종류는 무엇인가요?)

  • Have you ever waited a long time for a table?

    (식당 자리를 위해 오래 기다려본 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to make a reservation for ___.

    (___명 예약을 하고 싶습니다.)

  • Do you have a table for ___ tonight?

    (오늘 밤 ___명을 위한 자리가 있나요?)

  • The reservation is under the name ___.

    (예약자 이름은 ___입니다.)

  • How long is the wait?

    (대기 시간은 얼마나 되나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Can we get a table by the window? (창가 자리로 부탁할 수 있을까요?)

  • Do you have outside seating? (야외 좌석이 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In popular tourist cities, booking dinner is highly recommended. Many places fill up fast!

  • When you arrive, wait at the entrance until the host says: "Right this way." (안내해 드리겠습니다.)

Ordering Food (음식 주문하기)

Today's Goal: Order food and drinks confidently in a restaurant.
(오늘의 목표: 식당에서 자신감 있게 음식과 음료 주문하기)

Photo: A waiter holding a notepad, taking an order from a customer.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Are you ready to order quickly, or do you take your time?

    (주문을 빨리 하는 편인가요, 아니면 천천히 메뉴를 보는 편인가요?)

  • What do you usually drink with meals?

    (식사할 때 주로 어떤 음료를 마시나요?)

  • Have you ever not known what to order on a foreign menu?

    (외국 메뉴판을 보고 뭘 주문해야 할지 몰랐던 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to order the ___, please.

    (___로 주문할게요.)

  • Can I have ___ for now?

    (우선 ___ 좀 주실 수 있나요?)

  • I'll have the same.

    (같은 걸로 할게요.)

  • We need a few more minutes, please.

    (시간이 조금 더 필요합니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Could we get some extra napkins? (냅킨 좀 더 주실 수 있나요?)

  • What do you recommend? (무엇을 추천하시나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • "I'll have..." or "I'd like..." is much more polite than "Give me...".

  • In the US, servers check on you often ("Is everything tasting okay?"). Just smile and say "It's great!"

6. Talk More

  • What is your favorite international food?(가장 좋아하는 외국 음식은 무엇인가요?)

  • Do you like trying local food when traveling, or eating familiar food?(여행 시 현지 음식을 도전하는 편인가요, 아니면 익숙한 음식을 먹는 편인가요?)

Asking About the Menu (메뉴 및 알레르기 문의하기)

Today's Goal: Ask about ingredients and make special requests for your diet.
(오늘의 목표: 재료에 대해 묻고 식단에 맞춘 특별 요청하기)

Photo: A person pointing at a menu and asking the waiter a question.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you have any food allergies?

    (음식 알레르기가 있나요?)

  • Are you a picky eater?

    (음식을 가려 먹는(편식하는) 편인가요?)

  • Is there any ingredient you really hate? (e.g., cilantro, nuts)

    (정말 싫어하는 재료가 있나요? 예: 고수, 견과류 등)

  • Have you ever asked a restaurant to change a dish for you?

    (식당에 요리를 조금 변경해 달라고 요청해 본 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Does this dish have ___ in it?

    (이 요리에 ___가 들어가나요?)

  • Can you make it without ___?

    (___를 빼고 만들어 주실 수 있나요?)

  • I am allergic to ___.

    (저는 ___ 알레르기가 있습니다.)

  • Is this dish very spicy?

    (이 요리 많이 맵나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Do you have any vegetarian options? (채식주의자를 위한 메뉴가 있나요?)

  • Could you put the dressing on the side? (드레싱은 따로 담아 주실 수 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Servers in Western countries are very accustomed to allergy and dietary requests, so don't be shy to ask.

  • "Cilantro" is called "Coriander" in the UK and Australia.

Restaurant Problems (식당에서 문제 해결하기)

Today's Goal: Politely explain if there is an issue with your order or food.
(오늘의 목표: 주문이나 음식에 문제가 있을 때 정중하게 설명하기)

Photo: A customer politely talking to a waiter about their plate of food.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever received the wrong food at a restaurant?

    (식당에서 잘못된 음식을 받아본 적이 있나요?)

  • Did you complain or just eat it?

    (항의했나요, 아니면 그냥 먹었나요?)

  • What do you do if your food is too cold?

    (음식이 너무 차갑게 나오면 어떻게 하시나요?)

  • Is it difficult for you to complain in English?

    (영어로 불만을 제기하는 것이 어렵게 느껴지나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, this is not what I ordered.

    (실례합니다만, 이건 제가 주문한 게 아닙니다.)

  • I ordered the ___, not the ___.

    (저는 ___를 주문했고, ___가 아닙니다.)

  • My food is a little cold.

    (음식이 조금 차갑습니다.)

  • Could you check on our order?

    (저희 주문 좀 확인해 주시겠어요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • This tastes a bit too salty. (이거 맛이 조금 너무 짠 것 같아요.)

  • Could we get a replacement? (다른 것으로 다시 받을 수 있을까요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Always start with "Excuse me" and a smile. Mistakes happen in busy restaurants!

  • Waitstaff are usually very happy to replace the dish or heat it up for you.

Asking for the Bill and Tipping (계산서 요청 및 팁 주기)

Today's Goal: Ask for the check, split the bill, and understand tipping culture.
(오늘의 목표: 계산서를 요청하고 분할 결제를 하며 팁 문화 이해하기)

Photo: A person paying with a credit card at a restaurant table.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • How do you usually pay for meals when traveling?

    (여행할 때 식사비를 보통 어떻게 결제하시나요?)

  • Do people tip in your country?

    (당신의 나라에서는 팁을 주는 문화가 있나요?)

  • Do you usually split the bill when eating with friends?

    (친구들과 식사할 때 주로 더치페이(각자 계산)를 하시나요?)

  • Is it confusing to calculate tips abroad?

    (해외에서 팁을 계산하는 것이 헷갈리나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Could we get the bill, please?

    (계산서 좀 주시겠어요?)

  • Can we pay separately?

    (따로 결제할 수 있을까요?)

  • Is service included?

    (서비스 요금이 포함되어 있나요?)

  • Can I pay by card?

    (카드로 결제할 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Keep the change. (잔돈은 가지세요.)

  • Could I get a receipt? (영수증을 받을 수 있을까요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In the US and Canada, tipping 15-20% is standard. In Europe, it's often less or included in the bill.

  • In North America, you usually pay at the table. In Korea, you pay at the counter.

Server
