Looking for Items (물건 찾기)

Today's Goal: Ask store staff where to find specific items or just browse.
(오늘의 목표: 매장 직원에게 특정 물건의 위치를 묻거나 단순히 구경하기)

Photo: A customer looking at clothing racks in a bright store.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you like shopping when you travel?

    (여행할 때 쇼핑하는 것을 좋아하시나요?)

  • What do you usually buy abroad?

    (외국에서 주로 어떤 것을 사시나요?)

  • Have you ever gotten lost in a big mall?

    (큰 쇼핑몰에서 길을 잃은 적이 있나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm looking for ___.

    (저는 ___을(를) 찾고 있습니다.)

  • Do you sell ___?

    (여기에 ___을(를) 파나요?)

  • Where can I find ___?

    (___은(는) 어디서 찾을 수 있나요?)

  • I'm just looking, thank you.

    (그냥 구경하는 중이에요, 감사합니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Do you have this in a different color? (이거 다른 색상도 있나요?)

  • Could you help me find ___? (___ 찾는 것 좀 도와주시겠어요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In the US, staff often say "How are you doing today?" when you enter. Just say "Good, thanks!"

  • If a staff member asks "Can I help you?", and you don't need help, say "I'm just looking."

Asking About Prices & Discounts (가격과 할인 묻기)

Today's Goal: Ask for prices and check if there are any sales or discounts.
(오늘의 목표: 가격을 묻고 세일이나 할인이 있는지 확인하기)

Photo: A price tag showing a big discount on a clothing item.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you check prices carefully before you buy?

    (물건을 사기 전에 가격을 꼼꼼히 확인하시나요?)

  • Have you ever bought something too expensive by mistake?

    (실수로 너무 비싼 물건을 사본 적이 있나요?)

  • Do you like asking for discounts?

    (할인을 요구하는 것을 좋아하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • How much is this?

    (이거 얼마인가요?)

  • Is this on sale?

    (이거 세일 중인가요?)

  • Do you offer a tax refund?

    (택스리펀(세금 환급)이 되나요?)

  • That's a bit too expensive.

    (그건 조금 너무 비싸네요.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Can you give me a discount? (할인해 주실 수 있나요?)

  • Are there any special promotions today? (오늘 특별 프로모션이 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • 'Tax-free' shopping is very common in Europe and Japan. Always ask "Is there a tax refund?" and keep the receipt and your passport ready.

  • In the US, the price on the tag does NOT include tax. The final price at the register will be higher.

Trying Things On (옷 입어보기)

Today's Goal: Ask to use the fitting room and request different sizes.
(오늘의 목표: 피팅룸 사용을 묻고 다른 사이즈 요청하기)

Photo: A person holding hangers with clothes, standing near a fitting room.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you always try clothes on before buying?

    (옷을 사기 전에 항상 입어보시나요?)

  • Do you know your clothing size in the US or Europe?

    (미국이나 유럽에서 본인의 옷 사이즈를 알고 계신가요?)

  • What do you say if clothes are too small for you?

    (옷이 너무 작을 때 뭐라고 말하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Can I try this on?

    (이거 입어봐도 되나요?)

  • Where is the fitting room?

    (피팅룸(탈의실)이 어디에 있나요?)

  • Do you have this in a larger/smaller size?

    (이거 더 큰/작은 사이즈 있나요?)

  • It fits perfectly.

    (완벽하게 잘 맞습니다.)

  • It's too tight / too loose.

    (너무 꽉 끼어요 / 너무 헐렁해요.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Does this look good on me? (이거 저한테 잘 어울리나요?)

  • I'll take this one. (이걸로 할게요/살게요.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Sizes vary by country. For example, a US Women's size 4 is a UK size 8. Always ask to try it on!

  • In many Western stores, staff stand outside the fitting room to bring you different sizes. It's very helpful.

Returning or Exchanging Items (교환 및 환불)

Today's Goal: Learn how to return or exchange an item you bought.
(오늘의 목표: 구입한 물건을 교환하거나 환불하는 방법 배우기)

Photo: A customer talking to a cashier at the store counter with a shopping bag and receipt.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever returned something you bought?

    (구입한 물건을 반품해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Why did you return it?

    (왜 반품했나요?)

  • Is returning items easy in your country?

    (당신의 나라에서는 환불/반품이 쉬운 편인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to return this, please.

    (이거 반품(환불)하고 싶습니다.)

  • I'd like to exchange this for a different size.

    (이거 다른 사이즈로 교환하고 싶습니다.)

  • Here is my receipt.

    (제 영수증 여기 있습니다.)

  • There is a problem with this item.

    (이 물건에 문제가 좀 있습니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Can I get a full refund? (전액 환불받을 수 있을까요?)

  • The zipper is broken. (지퍼가 고장 났어요.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • You MUST have the original receipt to get a refund in most foreign countries. Keep it safe!

  • In the US, return policies are very generous (often 30 days), but you must leave the tags on the clothes.

Buying Souvenirs (기념품 사기)

Today's Goal: Talk about buying gifts and ask for recommendations at a souvenir shop.
(오늘의 목표: 기념품 상점에서 선물을 사고 추천받기)

Photo: A traveler looking at colorful local crafts and souvenirs in a market.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Who do you usually buy souvenirs for?

    (주로 누구를 위해 기념품을 사시나요?)

  • What is the best souvenir you have ever received?

    (지금까지 받아본 최고의 기념품은 무엇인가요?)

  • Do you prefer buying magnets, local snacks, or clothes?

    (자석, 현지 과자, 옷 중 어떤 것을 사는 것을 좋아하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'm looking for some souvenirs.

    (기념품을 좀 찾고 있습니다.)

  • What is the most popular item here?

    (여기서 가장 인기 있는 물건은 무엇인가요?)

  • I'll buy three of these.

    (이걸로 3개 살게요.)

  • Could you wrap them separately?

    (각각 따로 포장해 주실 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Are these locally made? (이것들은 현지에서 만들어진 건가요?)

  • This is a gift for my family. (이건 제 가족을 위한 선물입니다.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • "Could you wrap them separately?" is a very useful phrase when buying gifts for multiple friends.

  • At local markets, buying in bulk ("I'll take five of these") can often get you a small discount!

LESSON 30: Buying Souvenirs - Wrap-up & Assignment

6. Talk More
