Buying Tickets (기차/버스 표 구매하기)

Today's Goal: Buy tickets for public transportation and ask about prices.
(오늘의 목표: 대중교통 표를 구매하고 가격에 대해 묻기)

Photo: A traveler holding a train ticket or looking at a ticketing machine.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you prefer taking the train or the bus when traveling?

    (여행할 때 기차와 버스 중 어느 것을 선호하시나요?)

  • Have you ever bought a train ticket in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 기차표를 사본 적이 있나요?)

  • Do you usually buy tickets online or at the station?

    (보통 표를 온라인으로 예매하나요, 아니면 역에서 사나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like a ticket to ___, please.

    (___로 가는 표 한 장 부탁합니다.)

  • Single or return?

    (편도인가요, 왕복인가요?)

  • How much is it?

    (얼마인가요?)

  • Can I pay by card?

    (카드로 결제할 수 있나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • I'd like a window seat, if possible. (가능하다면 창가 좌석으로 부탁합니다.)

  • Are there any student discounts? (학생 할인이 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In the UK, they say "single" (편도) and "return" (왕복). In the US, they say "one-way" and "round-trip".

  • Keep your ticket safe! Controllers often check tickets on the train.

Asking About Schedules (시간표 문의하기)

Today's Goal: Ask for departure and arrival times and check platform numbers.
(오늘의 목표: 출발 및 도착 시간을 묻고 승강장 번호 확인하기)

Photo: A large electronic timetable board at a busy train station.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually check train schedules in advance?

    (주로 기차 시간표를 미리 확인하시나요?)

  • What happens if you miss your train?

    (기차를 놓치면 어떻게 되나요?)

  • Are trains usually on time in your country?

    (당신의 나라에서는 기차가 주로 제시간에 오나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • What time is the next train to ___?

    (다음 ___행 기차는 몇 시인가요?)

  • Which platform does it leave from?

    (어느 승강장에서 출발하나요?)

  • How long does the trip take?

    (여행(이동) 시간은 얼마나 걸리나요?)

  • What time does it arrive?

    (몇 시에 도착하나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Is the train delayed? (기차가 연착되었나요?)

  • How often do the buses run? (버스는 얼마나 자주 운행하나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In Europe, platform numbers are often announced only 10-15 minutes before departure.

  • Pay attention to 24-hour clocks (e.g., 15:00 instead of 3 PM) on timetables.

Renting a Car (렌터카 빌리기)

Today's Goal: Rent a car, ask about insurance, and confirm return policies.
(오늘의 목표: 차를 렌트하고, 보험에 대해 묻고, 반납 규정 확인하기)

Photo: A person receiving car keys at a rental car counter.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you enjoy driving?

    (운전하는 것을 즐기시나요?)

  • Have you ever rented a car during a trip?

    (여행 중에 차를 렌트해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Is it easy or difficult to drive in a foreign country?

    (외국에서 운전하는 것은 쉬운가요, 어려운가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to rent a car for ___ days.

    (___일 동안 차를 렌트하고 싶습니다.)

  • Do I need an international driver's license?

    (국제 운전면허증이 필요한가요?)

  • Is insurance included?

    (보험이 포함되어 있나요?)

  • Do I need to return it with a full tank of gas?

    (기름을 가득 채워서 반납해야 하나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • I prefer an automatic car. (오토(자동변속기) 차량을 선호합니다.)

  • Can I drop it off at the airport? (공항에 반납해도 될까요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In Europe, many rental cars are manual (stick shift). Ask for 'automatic' specifically.

  • Full coverage insurance is highly recommended when driving abroad.

Finding the Right Stop (올바른 정류장 찾기)

Today's Goal: Confirm you are at the correct bus stop or train platform.
(오늘의 목표: 당신이 올바른 버스 정류장이나 기차 플랫폼에 있는지 확인하기)

Photo: A traveler looking at a bus or train station sign.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever waited at the wrong bus stop?

    (잘못된 버스 정류장에서 기다려본 적이 있나요?)

  • How do you make sure you are going the right way?

    (올바른 방향으로 가고 있는지 어떻게 확인하시나요?)

  • Do you easily ask locals for help?

    (현지인들에게 쉽게 도움을 요청하는 편인가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Excuse me, does this bus go to ___?

    (실례합니다, 이 버스 ___로 가나요?)

  • Is this the right platform for ___?

    (여기가 ___로 가는 올바른 승강장이 맞나요?)

  • Am I on the right train?

    (제가 기차를 제대로 탄 건가요?)

  • Please let me know when we get there.

    (도착하면 좀 알려주세요.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • How many stops is it? (몇 정거장 남았나요?)

  • Is this seat taken? (이 자리 비어 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Asking the driver "Does this go to...?" as you board is the best way to not get lost.

  • If you ask another passenger to tell you when to get off, they are usually very helpful.

Navigating the Subway (지하철 이용 및 환승하기)

Today's Goal: Ask for subway directions and know how to transfer lines.
(오늘의 목표: 지하철 방향을 묻고 호선 환승하는 방법 알기)

Photo: A busy subway station with colorful lines and maps on the wall.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Is the subway system easy to use in your city?

    (당신이 사는 도시의 지하철은 이용하기 쉬운가요?)

  • Have you ever taken the subway in a foreign city like New York or London?

    (뉴욕이나 런던 같은 외국 도시에서 지하철을 타본 적이 있나요?)

  • What do you do if you take the train in the wrong direction?

    (기차를 반대 방향으로 탔다면 어떻게 하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Where is the nearest subway station?

    (가장 가까운 지하철역이 어디에 있나요?)

  • Which line do I need for ___?

    (___에 가려면 몇 호선을 타야 하나요?)

  • Take the Red Line.

    (레드 라인을 타세요.)

  • Where should I transfer?

    (어디서 환승해야 하나요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • You need to transfer at Central Station. (센트럴역에서 환승하셔야 합니다.)

  • Which exit is for the museum? (박물관으로 가려면 몇 번 출구인가요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Subways have different names: Subway (NY), Underground/Tube (London), Metro (Paris).

  • In many cities, subway lines are named by colors (Red Line) or letters/numbers (A Line, Line 1).
