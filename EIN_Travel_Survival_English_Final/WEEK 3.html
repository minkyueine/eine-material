Hotel Check-in (호텔 체크인)

Today's Goal: Check into a hotel, confirm your reservation, and ask about basic details.
(오늘의 목표: 호텔에 체크인하고 예약 확인 및 기본 정보 묻기)

Photo: A traveler holding a passport and speaking to a receptionist at a hotel front desk.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you prefer staying in hotels, hostels, or Airbnbs?

    (호텔, 호스텔, 에어비앤비 중 어디에 머무는 것을 선호하시나요?)

  • Have you ever had a problem during check-in?

    (체크인할 때 문제가 생긴 적이 있나요?)

  • What do you usually need to show when checking in?

    (체크인할 때 주로 무엇을 보여줘야 하나요?)

  • What kind of room do you usually book? (Double, Twin, Suite)

    (주로 어떤 종류의 객실을 예약하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to check in, please.

    (체크인하고 싶습니다.)

  • I have a reservation under the name ___.

    (___라는 이름으로 예약했습니다.)

  • Here is my passport and booking confirmation.

    (제 여권과 예약 확인서입니다.)

  • What is my room number?

    (제 방 번호는 몇 번인가요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Could I get a wake-up call at 7 AM? (오전 7시에 모닝콜을 부탁드릴 수 있을까요?)

  • Can I have two key cards? (객실 키 카드를 두 개 받을 수 있을까요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Hotels often ask for a credit card for a "deposit" (보증금) or "incidentals". Don't panic, it's refunded later.

  • If you arrive early before check-in time, you can usually leave your luggage at the front desk.

Requesting Room Amenities (객실 추가 물품 요청하기)

Today's Goal: Politely ask for extra items or services for your hotel room.
(오늘의 목표: 호텔 방에 필요한 추가 물품이나 서비스를 정중하게 요청하기)

Photo: A neatly made hotel bed with fresh white towels stacked on it.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What do you usually run out of in a hotel room?

    (호텔 방에서 주로 어떤 물건이 가장 먼저 떨어지나요?)

  • Have you ever called the front desk for more towels?

    (프론트 데스크에 수건을 더 달라고 전화해 본 적이 있나요?)

  • Do you drink the water or coffee provided in the room?

    (객실에 비치된 생수나 커피를 마시나요?)

  • Are you comfortable calling room service in English?

    (영어로 룸서비스에 전화하는 것이 편하신가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Could I get an extra ___, please?

    (여분의 ___를 받을 수 있을까요?)

  • I need more towels / water / hangers.

    (수건/물/옷걸이가 더 필요합니다.)

  • Can you bring it to room 412?

    (412호로 가져다주실 수 있나요?)

  • Do you have a/an ___ I could borrow?

    (제가 빌릴 수 있는 ___가 있을까요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Is there a charge for this? (이것에 대한 추가 요금이 있나요?)

  • We've run out of toilet paper. (화장지가 다 떨어졌습니다.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • In many hotels, basic amenities (towels, water, pillows) are free to request.

  • If you ask to borrow an iron or adapter, housekeeping usually brings it to your door.

Asking About Facilities (부대시설 문의하기)

Today's Goal: Ask questions about hotel facilities like breakfast, the pool, and Wi-Fi.
(오늘의 목표: 조식, 수영장, 와이파이 등 호텔 부대시설에 대해 묻기)

Photo: A beautiful hotel breakfast buffet spread.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Do you usually eat breakfast at the hotel?

    (호텔에서 주로 조식을 드시나요?)

  • What is your favorite hotel facility? (Pool, gym, spa?)

    (가장 좋아하는 호텔 부대시설은 무엇인가요? 수영장, 헬스장, 스파?)

  • Is free Wi-Fi important to you when choosing a hotel?

    (호텔을 고를 때 무료 와이파이가 중요한가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • Where is the breakfast room / pool / gym?

    (조식당 / 수영장 / 헬스장은 어디에 있나요?)

  • What time is breakfast served?

    (조식은 몇 시에 제공되나요?)

  • How do I access the Wi-Fi?

    (와이파이는 어떻게 접속하나요?)

  • It is on the ___ floor.

    (___층에 있습니다.)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Do I need a reservation for the spa? (스파를 이용하려면 예약이 필요한가요?)

  • Is the pool open late? (수영장은 늦게까지 여나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Breakfast times vary but usually end strictly around 10 AM. Don't be late!

  • Some hotels require you to bring your room key card to enter the gym or pool.

Handling Hotel Problems (객실 문제 해결하기)

Today's Goal: Explain a problem in your room and ask for a solution or a room change.
(오늘의 목표: 방의 문제점을 설명하고 해결책이나 방 교체를 요구하기)

Photo: A traveler holding a phone, looking stressed inside a hotel room.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • Have you ever had a dirty or noisy hotel room?

    (더럽거나 시끄러운 호텔 방을 배정받은 적이 있나요?)

  • What do you do if the air conditioning doesn't work?

    (에어컨이 작동하지 않으면 어떻게 하시나요?)

  • Are you comfortable complaining in a foreign language?

    (외국어로 컴플레인(불만 제기)을 하는 것이 편하신가요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • There is a problem with my room.

    (제 방에 문제가 있습니다.)

  • The AC / Wi-Fi / TV is not working.

    (에어컨 / 와이파이 / TV가 작동하지 않습니다.)

  • The room is too noisy / dirty.

    (방이 너무 시끄럽습니다 / 더럽습니다.)

  • Could I change rooms, please?

    (방을 바꿀 수 있을까요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Could you send someone to check it? (사람을 보내서 확인해 주실 수 있나요?)

  • The hot water isn't coming out. (뜨거운 물이 나오지 않아요.)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Be polite but firm. Start with 'Excuse me, there is a problem...'

  • If they can't fix it quickly, they will usually offer you a new room.

Checking Out & Luggage Storage (체크아웃 및 짐 보관)

Today's Goal: Check out of the hotel, pay the bill, and ask to store your luggage.
(오늘의 목표: 호텔 체크아웃, 결제, 그리고 짐 보관 요청하기)

Photo: A traveler leaving their bags with a hotel concierge.

1. Warm-up (워밍업 질문)

  • What time is check-out usually?

    (체크아웃 시간은 보통 몇 시인가요?)

  • Have you ever left your luggage at a hotel after checking out?

    (체크아웃 후 호텔에 짐을 맡겨본 적이 있나요?)

  • Do you usually ask the hotel to call a taxi for you?

    (주로 호텔에 택시를 불러달라고 요청하시나요?)

2. Speaking Patterns (핵심 패턴)

Core (Must-use)

  • I'd like to check out, please.

    (체크아웃하고 싶습니다.)

  • Here is my key.

    (제 객실 키 여기 있습니다.)

  • Everything was great.

    (모든 것이 좋았습니다.)

  • Can I leave my bags here?

    (여기에 짐을 맡길 수 있을까요?)

Upgrade (Optional)

  • Could I get a receipt, please? (영수증을 받을 수 있을까요?)

  • Could you call a taxi for me? (택시를 불러주실 수 있나요?)

Real-life Travel Tip

  • Most hotels allow free luggage storage on the day of check-out.

  • They will give you a 'baggage tag' – don't lose it to get your bags back!

6. Talk More

  • What is the first thing you do when you get home from a trip?(여행에서 집에 돌아오면 가장 먼저 하는 일은 무엇인가요?)

Tutor Tip
